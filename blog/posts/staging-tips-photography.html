Expert Staging Tips for Real Estate Photography

Andrew Boelman Professional Photographer

Proper staging is the foundation of exceptional real estate photography. The way a space is prepared and presented can make the difference between a good photo and a great one. In this comprehensive guide, we'll explore professional staging techniques that will elevate your real estate photography to the next level.

The Fundamentals of Staging

Before diving into specific rooms, let's cover the essential principles of staging for photography:

  • Declutter: Remove personal items and excess furniture
  • Clean: Ensure surfaces are spotless and windows are crystal clear
  • Symmetry: Create balanced compositions
  • Lighting: Maximize natural light and ensure all bulbs work
  • Flow: Create clear pathways through spaces

Room-by-Room Staging Guide

Living Room

  • Arrange furniture to create conversation areas
  • Add fresh throw pillows and blankets
  • Clear coffee tables except for styled accessories
  • Ensure window treatments are even
  • Add fresh flowers or plants for life

Kitchen

  • Clear countertops of appliances
  • Display minimal, attractive accessories
  • Remove fridge magnets and papers
  • Add a bowl of fresh fruit
  • Ensure cabinet handles align
"The goal of staging isn't to make a space look perfect, but to help potential buyers envision themselves living there."

Styling Tips for Photos

Consider these professional styling techniques:

  • Rule of Odds: Group decorative items in threes or fives
  • Color Theory: Use complementary colors in accessories
  • Texture: Mix different materials for visual interest
  • Scale: Vary the height of decorative elements
  • Negative Space: Don't overcrowd surfaces

Common Staging Mistakes

Avoid these frequent staging pitfalls:

  • Over-decorating spaces
  • Using dated or worn accessories
  • Blocking natural light
  • Pushing furniture against walls
  • Neglecting small details

Pre-Photo Checklist

Before taking photos, verify:

  1. All lights are working and same temperature
  2. Windows are clean and treatments even
  3. No personal items are visible
  4. Accessories are properly placed
  5. Floors and surfaces are spotless

Final Touches

Remember these last-minute details:

  • Turn on all lights
  • Open window treatments
  • Fluff pillows and straighten throws
  • Remove floor mats in bathrooms
  • Check for fingerprints on surfaces
