Home Staging Guide

Professional Tips for Staging Your Home for Photography

GENERAL PRINCIPLES

  • Declutter all spaces thoroughly
  • Clean every surface until it sparkles
  • Remove personal items and photos
  • Ensure all light bulbs work and are the same color temperature
  • Open all blinds/curtains to maximize natural light
  • Create clear pathways through each room
  • Remove seasonal decorations

ROOM-BY-ROOM GUIDE

Living Room
  • Remove excess furniture to show space
  • Arrange furniture to create conversation areas
  • Add fresh throw pillows and blankets
  • Clear coffee tables except for 1-2 decorative items
  • Hide TV remotes and cables
  • Clean windows and mirrors
Kitchen
  • Clear countertops of small appliances
  • Remove magnets and papers from refrigerator
  • Hide dish soap, sponges, and towels
  • Empty sink and dishwasher
  • Add a simple centerpiece to island/table
  • Clean all appliances until they shine
Bedrooms
  • Make beds with fresh, neutral linens
  • Remove items from nightstands
  • Clear floors of personal items
  • Organize closets (they may be photographed)
  • Remove exercise equipment
  • Ensure window treatments are even
Bathrooms
  • Clear countertops of personal items
  • Remove toiletries from shower/tub
  • Close toilet lids
  • Hang fresh, coordinated towels
  • Remove bath mats
  • Clean mirrors and glass surfaces

OUTDOOR SPACES

  • Mow lawn and trim edges
  • Remove vehicles from driveway
  • Store garbage bins out of sight
  • Clean outdoor furniture
  • Remove garden tools and hoses
  • Sweep walkways and driveway
  • Add fresh mulch to garden beds
Pro Tip: Stage your home as if you're preparing for a high-end magazine photoshoot. Less is more - aim for a clean, spacious feel that allows potential buyers to envision themselves in the space.
